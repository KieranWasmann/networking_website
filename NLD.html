Creating a Home Network

Network Diagram

This is a network diagram. It shows the connections and components of a basic home area network (HAN) that might be included in your own.

What are these components?

Modem

The modem is what allows the communication between devices and the external line. This is required because data is transferred over telephone lines in analog waves, while it is stored, read and processed on computers and devices digitally.

Router

The router carries out traffic direcing, which is the passing and receiving of packets of data over the internet between other routers.

Switch

The switch allows communication between devices within a network. It recieves packets then forwards them to the relevant devices.

Wireless Access Point (WAP)

The wireless acces point is what connects devices without a physical connection to your network. It creates your wifi, however it on its own does not have an internet connection

Desktop PC

A desktop PC is a computer, that is usually imovable, that can be connected wirelessly or with an ethernet cable. A ethernet cable is usually preffered as it is a lot faster.

Network Attached Storage (NAS)

Network attached storage allows the access and use of storage by all devices connected in a network.

Printer

A printer turns digital images and documents into physical things. Printers usually print on paper (in two dimensions) however three dimensional printers are becoming more and more available to the general public

802.11n

There have been several wireless protocols, with 802.11n being one of the latest, This is the first wireless protocol being to use 5ghz instead of 2.4.
